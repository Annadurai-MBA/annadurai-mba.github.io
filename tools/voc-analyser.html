Five sources processed

1

Quality team data

Field complaint summary Q2-Q3

⌄
Field complaint summary Q2-Q3: 18 incidents of sensor drift after CIP (cleaning in place) cycles using NaOH 2% at 80°C. Customers report recalibration required every 3 months instead of specified 12-month interval. Warranty claims concentrated in 3 food and beverage accounts. Root cause investigation ongoing — suspected diaphragm material degradation under alkaline cleaning chemistry.
2

Sales team CRM notes

Pharma, food processing, biotech accounts

⌄
Three separate accounts (pharma, food processing, biotech) requested FDA 21 CFR Part 11 compliant data logging this quarter. Current model does not support it. Lost one deal to a competitor explicitly on this basis. Two more accounts evaluating alternatives. Additional recurring request: HART protocol overlay on 4-20mA output — our model is 4-20mA only, no HART. Came up in 5 of last 8 technical discussions.
3

Business development notes

Market visit summary, DACH region

⌄
Market visit summary — 6 customer site visits across DACH region, pharmaceutical and biotech sector. Primary finding: validation lifecycle is the biggest procurement pain point. Customers report 8 to 14 weeks for sensor qualification per site, which creates significant project delays. Several mentioned IQ/OQ/PQ documentation package as a purchasing decision factor — currently not available from us. Two R&D managers mentioned interest in flush-mount diaphragm variant for viscous media applications. Not in current portfolio.
4

Market data and RFQ analysis

9 RFQs received in H1

⌄
Analysis of 9 RFQs received in H1: 7 of 9 specified ATEX Zone 1 certification requirement. Our current product is Zone 2 only. 6 of 9 required hygienic design (EHEDG or 3-A certification). 5 of 9 requested IO-Link or digital output option alongside 4-20mA. 4 of 9 required ingress protection IP69K — current product is IP67.
5

Direct customer interview

Process engineer, pharma contract manufacturer

⌄
Interview with process engineer, mid-size pharma contract manufacturer: Primary job is to ensure batch traceability compliance. Biggest pain is sensor exchange during production campaigns — every swap triggers a revalidation event. Estimated cost per revalidation: CHF 15,000 to 25,000 in downtime and documentation. Ideal: a sensor that can be exchanged without triggering full revalidation, with stored calibration certificate. Not aware of any supplier offering this currently. Secondary pain: installation — current threaded process connections require specialized tooling maintenance teams do not always have. Requested hygienic clamp connection as preferred option.

Buyer role analysis

Economic buyer

Operations Director / Head of Manufacturing

Driven by production uptime and revalidation cost avoidance. Decision weight increases significantly when CHF cost of revalidation is quantified.

Technical buyer

Process / Instrumentation Engineer

Driven by HART protocol, IO-Link, hygienic design compliance, IP69K, ATEX Zone 1. Most active in RFQ specification writing.

End user

Maintenance / Production team

Driven by installation ease, calibration interval, physical connection type. Often absent from procurement conversation but influences repeat purchase decisions.

Quality and regulatory

Quality Manager / Regulatory Affairs

Driven by FDA 21 CFR Part 11, IQ/OQ/PQ documentation, validation lifecycle management. Effective veto power in pharmaceutical sector.
