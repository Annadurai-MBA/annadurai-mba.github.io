ABOUT

Engineer by Training.
Builder by Nature.

I came to Switzerland as an engineer and stayed as a builder: of products, pipelines, and partnerships.

My career has moved in one consistent direction: from making things work technically, to making them work commercially. Starting with structural simulation in aerospace, I moved into project delivery in power and energy systems, product development in industrial connectors, and eventually led business development for advanced technologies, building a significant commercial pipeline across healthcare, automotive, and industrial sectors from a standing start.

What stays constant across every role is the ability to work at the intersection of engineering rigour and commercial instinct. I understand what customers actually need, not just what they ask for, and how to translate that into product decisions, project outcomes, and long-term partnerships.

I hold a PMP certification, an MBA from HSG St. Gallen, and an M.Sc. in Computational Engineering from Erlangen. I am Swiss, based in Aargau, and work fluently in English and German.

Avid reader of philosophy and historical fiction. Board member of a regional integration organisation, leading digital and financial operations. Increasingly interested in roles that connect European industrial expertise with India's manufacturing and technology growth.

Business Development Thin-film solar equipment manufacturing

Creating a New Revenue Stream in an Established Market

Context

The business model in this segment was built around equipment sales. Once a production line was installed and handed over, the commercial relationship largely ended, with no recurring revenue and no natural re-entry point for the supplier.

Situation

Installed customers had aging production systems with genuine upgrade needs, but no structured offering existed to address them. The commercial model to capture the opportunity had not been developed.

Approach

Worked across commercial and engineering teams to develop a retrofit service concept, including pricing rationale and customer value proposition. Participated in customer conversations to help validate the concept and coordinate interest with internal delivery capacity.

Outcome

The retrofit initiative contributed to establishing a new commercial offering for the installed customer base. The programme progressed from concept to funded business line, supporting a broader shift toward recurring commercial engagement.

Reflection

Early customer conversations moved quickly to commitment, which was useful. What would have helped more is investing earlier in a repeatable sales process. A structured playbook from the start would have made the programme easier to scale and less dependent on individual effort.

Project Management Global industrial engineering company

Zero Downtime. High Stakes. On Time.

Context

Medium-voltage drive modernisation projects ran inside active industrial facilities, including power generation, marine, and mining environments, where production interruption carried significant commercial and contractual consequences.

Situation

During commissioning on a large retrofit project, a scope dispute emerged. The customer indicated intent to withhold final payment, with time pressure on both sides.

Approach

Coordinated a structured response based on contract documentation, then worked directly with the customer's project lead to align on a resolution. A fair-value change order with an agreed delivery timeline helped close the gap. The situation was framed as a shared problem to work through rather than a contractual position to defend.

Outcome

The project closed on schedule without escalation. Consistent discipline around scope and cost across the portfolio contributed to improved margin outcomes over time.

Reflection

The dispute was partly preventable. Scope ambiguity in the original contract language was visible at order intake but not addressed firmly enough before signature. That experience made contract review a standing part of every project kick-off that followed.
