Business Development OC Oerlikon Solar

Creating a New Revenue Stream in an Established Market

Context

Oerlikon Solar's business model was built around equipment sales. Once a production line was installed and handed over, the commercial relationship largely ended, no recurring revenue, no natural re-entry point.

Problem

Installed customers had aging production systems with genuine upgrade needs, but no structured offering existed to address them. The opportunity was visible; the commercial model to capture it was not.

Approach

Developed the concept for a retrofit service offering, including the commercial framework, pricing rationale, and customer value proposition. Engaged directly with operations and engineering decision-makers at key accounts to validate the concept and convert interest into commitments. Worked closely with internal engineering teams to confirm delivery feasibility before making external commitments.

Result

The retrofit programme created a meaningful new revenue stream, with significant per-site contract value and a substantially larger addressable opportunity across the installed customer base. The programme moved from concept to funded business line within the engagement period.

Reflection

I moved quickly to close the first accounts, right instinct. But I underinvested in building a repeatable sales process early. A structured playbook from the start would have made the programme faster to scale and less dependent on individual effort. That lesson shaped how I structured pipeline work at Datwyler.

Project Management ABB Switzerland AG

Zero Downtime. High Stakes. On Time.

Context

Medium-voltage drive modernisation projects ran inside active industrial facilities: power plants, marine vessels, mining operations, where stopping production was commercially and contractually catastrophic.

Problem

During commissioning on a multi-million CHF retrofit project, a scope dispute emerged. The customer signalled intent to withhold final payment with time pressure mounting on both sides.

Approach

Returned to contract documentation before making any response. Established clearly what was and was not in scope, then met the customer's project lead directly on site, no intermediaries, no escalation. Walked through the documentation together and proposed a structured resolution: a fair-value change order with an accelerated delivery timeline. Framed as a joint problem to solve, not a position to defend.

Result

The dispute resolved without escalation. The project closed on time. A repeat order followed within six months, the relationship was demonstrably stronger after the pressure test than before. Disciplined cost and scope management contributed to sustained improvement in project EBIT margins across the portfolio.

Reflection

The dispute was partly preventable. Scope ambiguity in the original contract language was visible at order intake. I noted it but did not push hard enough for clarification before signature. That experience made contract review a standing agenda item in every project kick-off I have run since.
